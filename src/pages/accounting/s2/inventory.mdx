## Introduction

<Callout type="info">
  **<u>Article 12</u>** of Commercial Code:
  <blockquote>
    &ensp;**Goods Aquired** =
    <span className="highlight red">purchase cost</span> +
    <span className="highlight green">incidental purchase cost</span>

  </blockquote>

<blockquote>

## Average

<Callout type="error" emoji="‼️">
  THIS METHOD SHOULD BE USED AT THE END OF THE YEAR
</Callout>

<Steps>
### Weighted cost

The first step is to calculate the weighted cost of all input/acquisition goods.

<Callout type="info">
    <Latex>$\text\{Weighted Cost\} = \text\{Quantity\} \times \text\{Unit Cost\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

### Sum of weighted cost

Add all weighted cost.

### Total Input Quantity

Calculate the quantity of all inputs goods by adding all quantites of input/acquisition.

### Weighted Average

Calculate the weighted average cost.

<Callout type="info">
   <Latex>$\text\{Average\} = \frac\{\text\{Sum of weighted cost\}\}\{\text\{Total Quantity\}\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

### Consumption

Find the consumption cost.

<Callout type="info">
   <Latex>$\text\{Consumption Cost\} = \text\{Quantity Consumed\} \times \text\{Weighted Average\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

### Final Stock Value

Determine the final stock value

<Callout type="info">
    <Latex>$\text\{Final Stock Value\} = (\text\{Total Quantity\} - \text\{Consumed quantity\}) \times \text\{Weighted Average\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

</Steps>

## FIFO

<Callout type="error" emoji="‼️">
  THIS METHOD SHOULD BE USED FOR EACH EXIT TRANSACTION
</Callout>

<Steps>

### Inputs Used

The first step is to determine which and how many input goods are consumed.

### Weighted cost

Calculate the weighted cost of consumed goods

<Callout type="info">
    <Latex>$\text\{Weighted Cost\} = \text\{Quantity\} \times \text\{Unit Cost\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

### Consumption

Add all the weighted cost of consumed goods

### Final Stock Value

The final stock value is the sum of weighted unit cost of the remaining (not used) goods.

</Steps>

### Average

#### Step 1: Weighted Cost

  alt="Average Method Example 1 Image"
/>
<img
  className="light"
  src="/accounting/inventory/average-1-light.png"

Weighted cost are calculated in the specific column.<br/>

<Callout emoji="➕" type="info">
  <Latex>$200 \times 0,40€ = 80€$</Latex>
  <br />
  <Latex>$1000 \times 0,36€ = 360€$</Latex>
  <br />
  <Latex>$750 \times 0,33€ = 247,5€$</Latex>
  <br />
  <Latex>$600 \times 0,38€ = 228€$</Latex>
  <br />
  <Latex>$400 \times 0,36€ = 144€$</Latex>
  <br />
</Callout>
#### Step 2: Sum of Weighted Cost

We add all weighted cost. <br/>

<Callout emoji="➕" type="info">
    <Latex>$\text\{Sum\} = 80€ + 360€ + 247,5€ + 228€ + 144€ = 1059,5€$</Latex><br/>
</Callout>

#### Step 3: Total Quantity

We calculate the total acquired/input goods.<br/>

<Callout emoji="➕" type="info">
    <Latex>$\text\{Total Quantity\} = 200 + 1000 + 750 + 600 + 400 = 2950$</Latex><br/>
</Callout>

#### Step 4: Average Weighted Cost

We find the average with the average formula<br/>

<Callout emoji="➕" type="info">
    <Latex>$\text\{Average\} = \frac\{\text\{Sum of weighted cost\}\}\{\text\{Total Quantity\}\} = \frac\{\text\{1059,5\}\}\{\text\{2950\}\} = 0,359€ = 0,36€$</Latex>
</Callout>

#### Step 5: Consumption

Now that we have average weighted unit price, we can find the consumption.<br/>

<Callout emoji="➕" type="info">
    <Latex>$\text\{Consumption Cost\} = \text\{Quantity Consumed\} \times \text\{Weighted Average\} = 2500 \times 0,36€ = 900€$</Latex>
</Callout>

#### Step 6: Final Stock Value

The final stock value can thus be calculated:<br/>

<Callout emoji="➕" type="info">
 <Latex>$\text\{Final Stock Value\} \\\\ = (\text\{Total Quantity\} - \text\{Consumed quantity\}) \times \text\{Weighted Average\} \\\\ = (2950 - 2500) \times 0,36€ \\\\= 162€$</Latex>
 </Callout>

## Example 2

| Date         | Description          | Quantity | Unit Price |
| :----------- | :------------------- | :------- | :--------- |
| 1 September  | Initial stock        | 50       | 1,800€     |
| 5 September  | Exit voucher n.S451  | 30       | ???        |
| 7 September  | Entry voucher n.E920 | 140      | 1,950€     |
| 8 September  | Exit voucher n.S452  | 40       | ???        |
| 15 September | Exit voucher n.S453  | 54       | ???        |
| 17 September | Entry voucher n.E921 | 32       | 1,860€     |
| 21 September | Entry voucher n.E922 | 48       | 1,435€     |
| 26 September | Exit voucher n.S454  | 120      | ???        |

### Average

#### Step 1: Weighted Cost

We calculate the weighted cost for all acquired goods (all entry vouchers). 

<Callout emoji="➕" type="info">
<Latex>$50 \times 1,800€ = 90€$</Latex>
<br />
<Latex>$140 \times 1,950€ = 273,000€$</Latex>
<br />
<Latex>$32 \times 1,860€ = 59,520€$</Latex>
<br />
<Latex>$48 \times 1,435€ = 68,880€$</Latex>

</Callout>

#### Step 2: Sum of Weighted Cost

We add all the weighted cost to have the sum of weighted cost.

<Callout emoji="➕" type="info">
<Latex>$\text\{Sum of Weighted Cost\} = 90€ + 273,000€ + 59,520€ + 68,880€ = 491,400€$</Latex>
</Callout>

#### Step 3: Total Quantity

We add all the acquired quantities to have the total quantity.

<Callout emoji="➕" type="info">
<Latex>$\text\{Total Quantity\} = 50 + 140 + 32 + 48 = 270$</Latex>
</Callout>

#### Step 4: Average Weighted Cost 

We divide the sum of weighted cost by the total quantity to have the average weighted cost.

<Callout emoji="➕" type="info">
<Latex>$\text\{Average Weighted Cost\} = \frac\{491,400\}\{270\} = 1,820€$</Latex>
</Callout>

#### Step 5: Consumption

We calculate the consumption cost.

Total quality consumed is the sum of all exit quantities.

<Callout emoji="➕" type="info">
<Latex>$\text\{Quantity consumed\} = 30 + 40 + 54 + 120 = 244$</Latex>
<br/>
<Latex>$\text\{Consumption\} = 244 \times 1,820€ = 444,680€$</Latex>
</Callout>

#### Step 6: Final Stock Value

We calculate the final stock value.

<Callout emoji="➕" type="info">
<Latex>$\text\{Remaining quantity\} = 270 - 244 = 26$</Latex>
<br/>
<Latex>$\text\{Final Stock Value\} = 26 \times 1,820€ = 47,320€$</Latex>
</Callout>
