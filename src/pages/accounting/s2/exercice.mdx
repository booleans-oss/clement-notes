## Exercice 1

<Callout type="info">
    The Company LDS purchased office furniture on <span className="highlight">18 June</span> of year "N" for <span className="highlight">€7200</span> including
    tax, the VAT rate is <span className="highlight">20%</span>. The company uses <span className="highlight">linear amortization</span>. The useful life of the asset is
    <span className="highlight">five years</span>. The financial year-end is the end of December.
</Callout>
<br/>

<Steps>

### Step 1: Price Before Tax

<Callout type="info">
    Price Excluding Taxes = <Latex>$\frac\{\text\{Price Including Taxes\}\}\{1+\text\{VAT Rate\}\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

**Price Including Taxes** = €7200

**VAT Rate** = 20%

**Price Excluding Taxes** = <Latex>$\frac\{€7200\}\{1+20\\%\}$</Latex> = <span className="highlight red">€6000</span>

### Step 2: Rate

<Callout type="info">
    Linear Rate = <Latex>$\frac\{1\}\{\text\{Useful Life\}\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

**Useful Life** = 5 years

**Rate** = <Latex>$\frac\{1\}\{5\}$</Latex> = <span className="highlight red">0.2</span> = <span className="highlight red">20%</span>

### Step 3: Amortization

<Callout type="info">
    Amortization = <Latex>$\text\{Price Excluding Taxes\} \times \text\{Rate\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

**Amortization** = <Latex>$€6000 \times 20\\% = \frac\{€6000\}\{5\}$</Latex> = <span className="highlight red">€1200</span>

### Step 4: Used days

<Callout type="info">
    Used days = <Latex>$360 - \text\{Days from beginning of the year to purchase date\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

Since we bought the furniture on <span className="highlight">18 June</span>, we have to calculate the amortization for the first year.

**Used days** = <Latex>$360 - (30 \times 6 + 18) =$</Latex> <span className="highlight red">192 days</span>

### Step 5: Amortization for First Year

<Callout type="info">
    Amortization for First Year = <Latex>$\frac\{\text\{Amortization\}\}\{360\} \times \text\{Used days\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

**Amortization for First Year** = <Latex>$\frac\{€1200\}\{360\} \times 192 =$</Latex> <span className="highlight red">€640</span>

## Exercice 2

<Callout type="info">
**Invoice N°112 19/04/2023**

**Industrial tools**: €35 000

**VAT 20%** : €7 000

**Net to be paid** : €42 000

The equipment will be commissioned on 25 May. The depreciation period is 7 years (in degressive mode). 
</Callout>

<Steps>

### Step 1: Price Before Tax

The price before tax is already provided as it is €35 000. We can make sure by calculating it:

<Callout type="info">
    **Price Excluding Taxes** = <Latex>$\frac\{\text\{Price Including Taxes\}\}\{1+\text\{VAT Rate\}\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

**Price Excluding Taxes** = <Latex>$\frac\{€42 000\}\{1+20\\%\}$</Latex> = <span className="highlight red">€35 000</span>

### Step 2: Rates

<Callout type="info">
    **Linear Rate** = <Latex>$\frac\{1\}\{\text\{Useful Life\}\}$</Latex>

    **Degressive Annuity rate** = <Latex>$R_L \times \text\{Original Coefficient\} = \frac\{100\\%\}\{\text\{Duration of use\}\} \times \text\{Original Coefficient\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

<Callout type="error" emoji="‼️">
| Duration                                                                      | Original Coefficient |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------- | 
| Between <span className="highlight">3</span> and  <span className="highlight">4</span> years | 1.25          | 
| Between  <span className="highlight">5</span> and  <span className="highlight">6</span> years  | 1.75         |
| More than  <span className="highlight">6</span> years  | 2.25         |

</Callout>

**Linear Rate** = <Latex>$\frac\{1\}\{7\}$</Latex> = <span className="highlight red">0.14</span> = <span className="highlight red">14%</span>

**Degressive Annuity rate** = <Latex>$\frac\{100\\%\}\{7\} \times 2.25 =$</Latex> <span className="highlight red">32.14%</span>

### Step 3: Used months

<Callout type="info">
    **Used months** = <Latex>$12 - \text\{Months from beginning of the year to commissioning date\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

Since we commissioned the equipment on <span className="highlight">25 May</span>, we have to calculate the amortization for the first year.

**Used months** = <Latex>$12 - 4 =$</Latex> <span className="highlight red">8 months</span>

### Step 4: Amortization for First Year

<Callout type="info">
    **Amortization for First Year** = <Latex>$\text\{Price Excluding Taxes\} \times \text\{Degressive Rate\} \times \frac\{\text\{Used months\}\}\{12\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

**Amortization for First Year** = <Latex>$€35 000 \times 32.14\\% \times \frac\{8\}\{12\} =$</Latex> <span className="highlight red">€7499,33</span>
