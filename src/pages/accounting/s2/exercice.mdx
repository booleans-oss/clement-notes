## Exercice 1

<Callout type="info">
    The Company LDS purchased office furniture on <span className="highlight">18 June</span> of year "N" for <span className="highlight">€7200</span> including
    tax, the VAT rate is <span className="highlight">20%</span>. The company uses <span className="highlight">linear amortization</span>. The useful life of the asset is
    <span className="highlight">five years</span>. The financial year-end is the end of December.
</Callout>
<br/>

<Steps>

### Step 1: Price Before Tax

<Callout type="info">
    Price Excluding Taxes = <Latex>$\frac\{\text\{Price Including Taxes\}\}\{1+\text\{VAT Rate\}\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

**Price Including Taxes** = €7200

**VAT Rate** = 20%

**Price Excluding Taxes** = <Latex>$\frac\{€7200\}\{1+20\\%\}$</Latex> = <span className="highlight red">€6000</span>

### Step 2: Rate

<Callout type="info">
    Linear Rate = <Latex>$\frac\{1\}\{\text\{Useful Life\}\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

**Useful Life** = 5 years

**Rate** = <Latex>$\frac\{1\}\{5\}$</Latex> = <span className="highlight red">0.2</span> = <span className="highlight red">20%</span>

### Step 3: Amortization

<Callout type="info">
    Amortization = <Latex>$\text\{Price Excluding Taxes\} \times \text\{Rate\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

**Amortization** = <Latex>$€6000 \times 20\\% = \frac\{€6000\}\{5\}$</Latex> = <span className="highlight red">€1200</span>

### Step 4: Used days

<Callout type="info">
    Used days = <Latex>$360 - \text\{Days from beginning of the year to purchase date\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

Since we bought the furniture on <span className="highlight">18 June</span>, we have to calculate the amortization for the first year.

**Used days** = <Latex>$360 - (30 \times 6 + 18) =$</Latex> <span className="highlight red">192 days</span>

### Step 5: Amortization for First Year

<Callout type="info">
    Amortization for First Year = <Latex>$\frac\{\text\{Amortization\}\}\{360\} \times \text\{Used days\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

**Amortization for First Year** = <Latex>$\frac\{€1200\}\{360\} \times 192 =$</Latex> <span className="highlight red">€640</span>

## Exercice 2

<Callout type="info">
**Invoice N°112 19/04/2023**

**Industrial tools**: €35 000

**VAT 20%** : €7 000

**Net to be paid** : €42 000

The equipment will be commissioned on 25 May. The depreciation period is 7 years (in degressive mode). 
</Callout>

<Steps>

### Step 1: Price Before Tax

The price before tax is already provided as it is €35 000. We can make sure by calculating it:

<Callout type="info">
    **Price Excluding Taxes** = <Latex>$\frac\{\text\{Price Including Taxes\}\}\{1+\text\{VAT Rate\}\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

**Price Excluding Taxes** = <Latex>$\frac\{€42 000\}\{1+20\\%\}$</Latex> = <span className="highlight red">€35 000</span>

### Step 2: Rates

<Callout type="info">
    **Linear Rate** = <Latex>$\frac\{1\}\{\text\{Useful Life\}\}$</Latex>

    **Degressive Annuity rate** = <Latex>$R_L \times \text\{Original Coefficient\} = \frac\{100\\%\}\{\text\{Duration of use\}\} \times \text\{Original Coefficient\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

<Callout type="error" emoji="‼️">
| Duration                                                                      | Original Coefficient |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------- | 
| Between <span className="highlight">3</span> and  <span className="highlight">4</span> years | 1.25          | 
| Between  <span className="highlight">5</span> and  <span className="highlight">6</span> years  | 1.75         |
| More than  <span className="highlight">6</span> years  | 2.25         |

</Callout>

**Linear Rate** = <Latex>$\frac\{1\}\{7\}$</Latex> = <span className="highlight red">0.14</span> = <span className="highlight red">14%</span>

**Degressive Annuity rate** = <Latex>$\frac\{100\\%\}\{7\} \times 2.25 =$</Latex> <span className="highlight red">32.14%</span>

### Step 3: Used months

<Callout type="info">
    **Used months** = <Latex>$12 - \text\{Months from beginning of the year to commissioning date\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

Since we commissioned the equipment on <span className="highlight">25 May</span>, we have to calculate the amortization for the first year.

**Used months** = <Latex>$12 - 4 =$</Latex> <span className="highlight red">8 months</span>

### Step 4: Amortization for First Year

<Callout type="info">
    **Amortization for First Year** = <Latex>$\text\{Price Excluding Taxes\} \times \text\{Degressive Rate\} \times \frac\{\text\{Used months\}\}\{12\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

**Amortization for First Year** = <Latex>$€35 000 \times 32.14\\% \times \frac\{8\}\{12\} =$</Latex> <span className="highlight red">€7499,33</span>

### Step 5: Amortization Table

<Callout type="info">
     **Compared Linear Annuity rate** = <Latex>$\frac\{100\\%\}\{\text\{Remaining years\}\} = \frac\{100\\%\}\{\text\{N - \text\{Current year\}\}\} = R_\{CL\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

## Exercice 3

<Callout type="info">
- Garel: amount of the receivable <span className="highlight">8,820 €</span> including tax (VAT 20%). We expect to recover
<span className="highlight">70%</span> of the debt.

- Pépin: amount of the receivable <span className="highlight">6,024 €</span> including tax (VAT 20%). Evaluation of the
probable loss: <span className="highlight">40%</span>.
</Callout>

<Steps>

### Step 1: Amount of the Receivable

<Callout type="info">
    **Amount of the Receivable** = <Latex>$\frac\{\text\{Amount of the Receivable Including Taxes\}\}\{1+\text\{VAT Rate\}\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

**Amount of the Receivable for Garel** = <Latex>$\frac\{€8 820\}\{1+20\\%\}$</Latex> = <span className="highlight red">€7 350</span>

**Amount of the Receivable for Pepin** = <Latex>$\frac\{€6 024\}\{1+20\\%\}$</Latex> = <span className="highlight red">€5 020</span>

### Step 2: Provision

<Callout type="info">
    **Provision** = <Latex>$\text\{Amount of the Receivable\} \times \text\{Percentage of the possible loss\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

For Garel, the provision is the opposite of what we can recover, thus: <span className="highlight">30%</span>. (100% - 70%)

**Provision for Garel** = <Latex>$€7 350 \times 30\\% =$</Latex> <span className="highlight red">€2 205</span>

For Pepin, the provision is the percentage of the possible loss, thus: <span className="highlight">40%</span>.

**Provision for Pepin** = <Latex>$€5 020 \times 40\\% =$</Latex> <span className="highlight red">€2 008</span>

### Step 3: Recording

Account for Depreciation, Amortization and Provision = <span className="highlight green">68</span>

Account for provision for receivables = <span className="highlight green">49</span>

### Customer A

#### Step 1: Amount After Payment

<Callout type="info">
    **Amount After Payment** = <Latex>$\text\{Amount of the Receivable Including Taxes\} - \text\{Payment\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

**Amount After Payment for Customer A** = <Latex>$€10 080 - €5 382 =$</Latex> <span className="highlight red">€4 698</span>

#### Step 2: Amount excluding VAT

<Callout type="info">
    **Amount excluding VAT** = <Latex>$\frac\{\text\{Amount After Payment\}\}\{1+\text\{VAT Rate\}\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

**Amount excluding VAT for Customer A** = <Latex>$\frac\{€4 698\}\{1+20\\%\} =$</Latex> <span className="highlight red">€3 915</span>

#### Step 3: Existing Provision

<Callout type="info">
    **Existing Provision** = <Latex>$\text\{Amount excluding VAT\} \times \text\{Percentage of the existing provision\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

**Existing Provision for Customer A** = <Latex>$€8400 \times 30\\% =$</Latex> <span className="highlight red">€2 520</span>

#### Step 4: Loss

Since it is the last payment, the loss is the remaining amount after payment (excl. taxes) that the customer will never pay back.

**Loss** = <span className="highlight red">€3 915</span>

#### Step 5: Loss VAT

<Callout type="info">
    **Loss VAT** = <Latex>$\text\{Loss\} \times \text\{VAT Rate\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

**Loss VAT for Customer A** = <Latex>$€3 915 \times 20\\% =$</Latex> <span className="highlight red">€783</span>

#### Step 6: Provision Needed

Since we declared a loss, the provision needed is now <span className="highlight red">0</span>.

#### Step 7: Depreciation or Reversal

The existing provision is <span className="highlight red">€2 520</span> and the provision needed is <span className="highlight red">0</span>. Thus, we need to reverse the provision (needed < existing).

### Customer B

#### Step 1: Amount After Payment

<Callout type="info">
    **Amount After Payment** = <Latex>$\text\{Amount of the Receivable Including Taxes\} - \text\{Payment\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

**Amount After Payment for Customer B** = <Latex>$€3 120 - €598 =$</Latex> <span className="highlight red">€2 522</span>

#### Step 2: Amount excluding VAT

<Callout type="info">
    **Amount excluding VAT** = <Latex>$\frac\{\text\{Amount After Payment\}\}\{1+\text\{VAT Rate\}\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

**Amount excluding VAT for Customer B** = <Latex>$\frac\{€2 522\}\{1+20\\%\} =$</Latex> <span className="highlight red">€2 101,67</span>

#### Step 3: Existing Provision

<Callout type="info">
    **Existing Provision** = <Latex>$\text\{Amount excluding VAT\} \times \text\{Percentage of the existing provision\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

**Existing Provision for Customer B** = <Latex>$€2 600 \times 15\\% =$</Latex> <span className="highlight red">€390</span>

#### Step 4: Loss

Since it is not the last payment, there is no loss.

#### Step 5: Provision Needed

<Callout type="info">
    **Provision Needed** = <Latex>$\text\{Amount excluding VAT\} \times \text\{Percentage of the updated provision\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

**Provision Needed for Customer B** = <Latex>$€2 101,67 \times 30\\% =$</Latex> <span className="highlight red">€630,50</span>

#### Step 6: Depreciation or Reversal

The existing provision is <span className="highlight red">€390</span> and the provision needed is <span className="highlight red">€630,50</span>. Thus, we need to depreciate the provision (needed > existing).
