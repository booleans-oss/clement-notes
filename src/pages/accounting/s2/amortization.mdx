## Amortization

- Recognition of a decline in the value of an assets
- **NON-CASH** expense in the income statement
- calculated expense

## Depreciable asset

- tangible assets (except land, work of art, and historical artifacts)
- intangible assets (patents, licenses, softwares)

<Callout type="error" emoji="‼️">
  **Financial assets = not depreciable**
</Callout>

## Authorization period

**Period of use of the asset**

- **Equipment**: between <span className="highlight">6</span> and <span className="highlight">10</span> years
- **Transport equipment**: between <span className="highlight">4</span> and <span className="highlight">5</span> years
- **Software & Hardware**: <span className="highlight">3</span> years

## Amortization Schedule

- Original Value (cost of the asset <span className="highlight red">excluding VAT</span>)
- Amortization annuity (amount of the amortization expense each year)
- Normal duration of use (number of years of use of the asset fixed by the fiscal authorities)
- Amortization rate: <span className="highlight">100% / Normal duration of use</span>
- Net Book Value: <span className="highlight">Original Value - Cumulative amortization annuities</span> = <span className="highlight red">annual depreciation</span>

## Example

Purchase of a machine 25000€, duration of use 5 years

- <span className="highlight">Case 1</span>: date = 1 January N{" "}
- <span className="highlight">Case 2</span>: date = 12 August N{" "}

### Linear method

#### Case 1

<Callout type="info">
  **Annuity rate** = <Latex>$\frac\{100\\%\}\{\text\{Duration of use\}\}$</Latex><br/>
  **Amortization annuity** = <Latex>$\text\{Annuity rate\} \times \text\{Original Value\}$</Latex> <br/>
  **Net Book Value** = <Latex>$\text\{Original Value\} - \text\{Cumulated annuities\}$</Latex><br/>
  or <br/>
  **Net Book Value** = <Latex>$\text\{Depreciable Base\} - \text\{Annuity\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

**Amortization rate**<Latex> = $\frac\{100\\%\}\{5\}=20\\%$</Latex><br/>
**Amortization annuity**<Latex> = $20\\%\times25000€ = 5000€$</Latex>

| Year | Depreciable base | Annuity | Cumulated annuities | Net Book Value |
| ---- | ---------------- | ------- | ------------------- | -------------- |
| N    | 25000            | 5000    | 5000                | 20000          |
| N+1  | 20000            | 5000    | 10000               | 15000          |
| N+2  | 15000            | 5000    | 15000               | 10000          |
| N+3  | 10000            | 5000    | 20000               | 5000           |
| N+4  | 5000             | 5000    | 25000               | 0              |

#### Case 2

<Callout type="info">
  **Annuity rate** = <Latex>$\frac\{100\\%\}\{\text\{Duration of use\}\}$</Latex><br/>
  **Amortization annuity** = <Latex>$\text\{Annuity rate\} \times \text\{Original Value\}$</Latex> <br/>
  **Net Book Value** = <Latex>$\text\{Original Value\} - \text\{Cumulated annuities\}$</Latex><br/>
  or <br/>
  **Net Book Value** = <Latex>$\text\{Depreciable Base\} - \text\{Annuity\}$</Latex><br/>
  **Annuity for the first year** = <Latex>$\text\{Annuity rate\} \times \text\{Original Value\} \times \frac\{\text\{Number of used days\}\}\{360\}$</Latex>
</Callout>

**Amortization rate**<Latex> = $\frac\{100\\%\}\{5\}=20\\%$</Latex><br/>
**Amortization annuity**<Latex> = $20\\%\times25000€ = 5000€$</Latex><br/>
**Annuity for the first year**<Latex> = $20\\%\times25000€ \times \frac\{4\times30+(30-12)\}\{360\} = 1916,66€$</Latex>

### Degressive method

#### Case 1

<Callout type="error" emoji="‼️">
| Duration                                                                      | Original Coefficient |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------- | 
| Between <span className="highlight">3</span> and  <span className="highlight">4</span> years | 1.25          | 
| Between  <span className="highlight">5</span> and  <span className="highlight">6</span> years  | 1.75         |
| More than  <span className="highlight">6</span> years  | 2.25         |

</Callout>

<Callout type="info">
  **Linear Annuity rate** = <Latex>$\frac\{100\\%\}\{\text\{Duration of use\}\} = R_L$</Latex>

**Compared Linear Annuity rate** = <Latex>$\frac\{100\\%\}\{\text\{Remaining years\}\} = \frac\{100\\%\}\{\text\{N - \text\{Current year\}\}\} = R_\{CL\}$</Latex>

**Degressive Annuity rate** = <Latex>$R_L \times \text\{Original Coefficient\} = \frac\{100\\%\}\{\text\{Duration of use\}\} \times \text\{Original Coefficient\}$</Latex>

**Amortization annuity** = <Latex>$\text\{Annuity rate\} \times \text\{Original Value\}$</Latex>

**Net Book Value** = <Latex>$\text\{Original Value\} - \text\{Cumulated annuities\}$</Latex>

or

**Net Book Value** = <Latex>$\text\{Depreciable Base\} - \text\{Annuity\}$</Latex>

</Callout>

**Linear Amortization rate**<Latex> = $\frac\{100\\%\}\{5\}=20\\%$</Latex><br/>

**Degressive Annuity rate**<Latex> = $20\\%\times1.75 = 35\\%$</Latex><br/>
