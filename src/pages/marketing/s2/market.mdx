## Porter's Five Forces

<Callout type="info">
  **Porter's Five Forces** is a tool to analyze the competition in a specific
  market.
</Callout>

<img
  className="light"
  src="/marketing/intl-marketing/porter-matrix-light.png"
  alt="Porter's Five Forces Image"
/>

<img
  className="dark"
  src="/marketing/intl-marketing/porter-matrix-dark.png"
  alt="Porter's Five Forces Image"
/>

### Current competition

- How many competitors are there?
- How big are they?
- How many products do they have?
- How much do they charge for their products/services?

### Potential competition

- How easy is it to enter the market?
- Is there place for new competitors?
- How much would it cost to enter the market?
- How long would it take to enter the market?

### Substitute products

- Are there any substitute products?
- How much do they cost?
- How many people use them?

### Supplier power

- How many suppliers are there?
- How big are they?
- How many products do they have?
- How much do they charge for their products/services?

### Customer power

- How many customers are there?
- How big are they?
- How many products do they buy?
- How much do they pay for the products/services?

## Kraljic Matrix

<Callout type="info">

<img

### Strategic items

- <span class="highlight green font-bold">High</span> profit impact
- <span class="highlight green font-bold">High</span> supply risk
- Development of long-term relationships
- Collaboration & Innovation
- Natural scarcity

### Leverage items

- <span class="highlight green font-bold">High</span> profit impact
- <span class="highlight red font-bold">Low</span> supply risk
- Full purchasing power
- Targeted pricing strategies
- Abundant supply

### Bottleneck items

- <span class="highlight red font-bold">Low</span> profit impact
- <span class="highlight green font-bold">High</span> supply risk
- <span class="highlight red font-bold">Low</span> control of suppliers
- Innovation & Production Substitution
- Production-based scarcity

### Non-critical items

- <span class="highlight red font-bold">Low</span> profit impact
- <span class="highlight red font-bold">Low</span> supply risk
- Standardization & Simplification
- Process efficiency
- Abundant supply

## Ansoff Matrix

<Callout type="info">
  **Ansoff Matrix** is a tool to analyze the growth of a company.
</Callout>

<img
  className="light"
  src="/marketing/intl-marketing/ansoff-matrix-light.png"
  alt="Ansoff Matrix Image"
/>

<img
  className="dark"
  src="/marketing/intl-marketing/ansoff-matrix-dark.png"
  alt="Ansoff Matrix Image"
/>

### Market penetration

- <span class="highlight green">LEAST RISK</span>
- Sell more of the same product to the same market
- Increase market share

### Market development

- <span class="highlight green">LEAST RISK</span>
- Sell the same product to a new market
- Enter new markets

### Product development

- <span class="highlight orange">MEDIUM RISK</span>
- Sell a new product to the same market
- Develop new products

### Diversification

- <span class="highlight red">HIGHEST RISK</span>
- Sell a new product to a new market
- Enter new markets with new products
